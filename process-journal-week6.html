Interaction Design Process

Week 6

Thursday, 6th of August, 2018

Tutorial & Studio

Tutorial:

During the tutorial, we had a guest from Small Multiples where we had the opportunity to learn about UI wireframing, and how paper/rapid prototyping is very useful for testing lofi concepts and understanding user feedback and behaviour. We were provided with a variety of tips and tricks in doing this, including utilising fake phones to replicate how the UI would appear in context for the user.
This tutorial was extremely helpful in understanding how the lo-fi prototyping should be executed for our own users and autonomous vehicle usage.

Studio:

During the studio, we finalised the brief to completion along with the finalisation of our problem scope decision. Before delving straight into the solution, we aimed to produce a variety of concepts, as crazy and as many as we could develop for our next group meeting. Furthermore, we wanted to be able to come together and potentially combine our ideas or choose an idea from the stack that we would have. Following this, we decided to visualise our 3 favourite concepts as either storyboards, or user journey maps - whichever we were more inclined to producing. This would allow for us to go through as many concepts as we can, iterate and refine before coming to the innovative solution for our senior citizen users. Reflection and next steps:
Complete what is required before meeting up on Wednesday!

Reflection:

  • Produce concepts
  • Produce storyboards/user journey maps!!!
  • Hopefully conclude with a concept by the next meeting so that we can start creating lo-fi prototypes and start testing with users
