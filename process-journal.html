Product Design Process

What's this all about?

This design process journal followed the journey of a 12 week process with a strong focus on interaction design and user interface design along with the foundation of user experience and design thinking. Essentially, this design process journal demonstrates the process from beginning of a design project to completion, demonstrating the highs and lows as well the iterative process guided by user testing and research. By the end of this project, we had two final mobile and tablet applications with two distinct user groups that were designed to work together and ensure for a greater user experience for all users involved. I hope you enjoy the 12 weeks I experienced for the design process and in sharing my exciting and difficult moments, as well as my passion for design growing.

Week 1

Thursday, 2nd of August, 2018

Tutorial & Studio

In short:

1. Learned sketching and practiced sketching during the tutorial.

2. Formed groups - got into a group with Connor Meehan.

3. Completed the ‘Group Charter’ outlining the T&C’s of being in a working and functional group.

4. Read through the design brief in order to understand the task at hand.

5. Started brainstorming and begun the design process for DECO2200.

During the Studio: Connor and I began talking about what does autonomous mean to us and our community? Initially, we simply considered the face value aspects of autonomous; such as self driving vehicles and UAV’s (unmanned aerial vehicle) or in general, drones. However, as we continued to explore the concept of autonomous, we began to realise there was greater elements are divergent concepts that also had potential. From this realisation, we started to form a brainstorming session and mind mapped a large variety of different autonomous concepts aligned with different user groups we would have the opportunity to explore. (mind map picture here) Due to the large variety of ideas, our goals aligned to wishing to learn more about the user groups specifically in public transport, emergency systems (medicine), disabled/seniors, and homeless users. I will be researching the autonomous vehicle usage for rapid emergency medical aid and distributing food and clothing to Sydney’s homeless. Whilst Connor will be researching the application of autonomous vehicles to aid in the transportation of elderly and disabled citizens as well as on-demand autonomous vehicles as a solution to congestion in Sydney without trains.

Goal for next week: Meetup on Wednesday the 8th of August to go over our individual research on our assigned user groups. Work throughout the tutorial to continue our research and progressively move forward with our project. Reflection This week was very productive and allowed us to understand our wants and needs and the tasks we have assigned for ourselves. We are excited for this semester.
